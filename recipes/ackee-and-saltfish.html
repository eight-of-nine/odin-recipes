Jamaican Ackee and Saltfish

Description

Ackee and saltfish is Jamaica's National dish, full of flavor and cultural importance. It contains ackee, a yellow fruit with a mild taste, and salted codfish, which provides it with a savory flavor.

Ingredients

  • Canoloa oil
  • Yellow onion
  • Green scallion
  • Fresh thyme
  • Scotch bonnet pepper
  • Salted cod fish
  • Tomato
  • Ackee
  • Black pepper

Steps

  1. First, prepare your saltfish by either soaking it in water overnight, draining it, and then boiling it once, and then draining it again. You can also skip the overnight soak and boil and drain the salted fish 2-3 times until the excess salt is removed. Once the saltfish has been boiled and drained, break the saltfish into small pieces. Set aside.
  2. Heat oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add onions, scallions, thyme, and scotch bonnet to skillet and saut√© for 3-4 minutes, until onions have softened. Add salted fish and stir for an additional minute.
  3. Serve and enjoy
