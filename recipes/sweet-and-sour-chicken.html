Sweet & Sour Chicken

Description

We’ve got crispy yet juicy chicken with a tangy sauce that’s perfectly balanced with just the right amount of sweetness. Want to know the secret to that irresistible crispy coating and the sauce that will have everyone asking for seconds? This is my homemade crispy sweet and sour chicken with a sweet and tangy sauce made from scratch using store cupboard ingredients!

Ingredients

  • Vegetable oil
  • 2 eggs lightly beaten
  • Cornflour
  • Plain flour
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Garlic
  • Paprika
  • Skinless chicken breast fillets
  • Onion (large)
  • Red pepper
  • Green pepper
  • 2 cloves of garlic
  • Minced ginger
  • Tomato Ketchup
  • Malt vinegar
  • Dark brown muscovado sugar
  • Canned pineapple

Steps

  1. Heat the oil in a wok or large frying pan until very hot. (5 tbsp vegetable oil)
  2. Whilst the oil is heating, place the egg in one shallow bowl and the cornflour in another shallow bowl. Add the flour, salt, pepper, garlic salt and paprika to another shallow bowl and mix together. (2 eggs lightly beaten,3 tbsp cornflour (cornstarch),90 g (3/4 cup) plain flour (all-purpose flour),½ tsp salt,½ tsp pepper,½ tsp garlic salt,2 tsp paprika)
  3. Dredge the chicken in the cornflour, then dip it in the egg (make sure all of the chicken is covered in egg wash), and finally dredge it in the seasoned flour. Repeat until you've coated all the chicken. (3 skinless chicken breast fillets)
  4. Add to the wok and cook on a high heat for 6-7 minutes, turning two or three times during cooking, until well browned. You may need to cook in two batches (I find I can do it in one batch so long as it's no more than 3 chicken breasts).
  5. Check the chicken is cooked (see notes) and remove from the pan and place in a bowl lined with kitchen towels.
  6. Whilst the chicken is cooking, you can start on your sauce.
  7. Add the oil to a large frying pan/skillet, heat it up on a medium-to-high heat and add in the onions and the peppers . Cook for 3 minutes, stirring every now and then, until the onions start to go translucent. (1 tbsp vegetable oil,1 large onion,1 red pepper,1 green pepper)
  8. Add in the garlic and ginger and cook for another minute. (2 cloves of garlic,1 tsp minced ginger)
  9. Add the tomato ketchup, vinegar, dark brown sugar, and the tinned pineapple (including the juice) and stir. Bring to the boil, and then turn down the heat and let it bubble, stirring every so often, until slightly thickened, should take 3-5 minutes. (150 ml (2/3 cup) tomato ketchup,2 tbsp malt vinegar,6 tbsp dark brown muscovado sugar,475 g (17 oz) canned pineapple chunks in juice)
  10. Transfer the cooked crispy chicken to the pan with the sauce and gently toss to coat.
  11. Serve with boiled or fried rice and a sprinkling of sesame seeds. (egg fried rice or boiled rice,1 tsp sesame seeds)
